## Part 1: The Foundation

Before you touch an ad account, before you write a single line of copy, before you open Canva or Klaviyo or Meta, you need to understand one thing:

**We are not an agency that runs ads. We are a machine that makes customers profitable.**

## Part 2: The Five Laws

These are non-negotiable. They apply to every client, every vertical, every budget size.

### Law 1: Know the Math Before You Spend the Money

*"I'm calculating everything based on unit economics that you guys shared with me in the very beginning."*

Before a single dollar of ad spend goes live, you must know:

- **Average Order Value (AOV)** — what a customer spends per transaction  
- **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)** — what the product costs to make and ship  
- **Contribution Margin** — what's left after COGS and fulfillment  
- **Breakeven CPA** — the maximum you can pay to acquire a customer and not lose money  
- **LTV:CAC Ratio** — lifetime value divided by acquisition cost. Below 2:1 is a warning. Below 1:1 is a fire.

*"The only reason why I'm spending is not for the sake of spending more blindly. It's spend more with profitability in mind."*

**The Unit Economics Calculator is mandatory for every new client.** Not optional. Not "we'll get to it." It happens in Week 1, during onboarding. If the math doesn't work, we tell the client. We don't burn their money to collect our retainer.

**What this looks like in practice:**

1. Client provides COGS, shipping costs, and average margins  
2. You build the breakeven CPA: (AOV × margin) \= max CPA  
3. You set a target CPA at 70-80% of breakeven (profit buffer)  
4. Every campaign decision filters through this number  
5. If you can't hit target CPA after 2 weeks of testing, something structural is wrong. Escalate.

### Law 2: Creative Is the Variable. Everything Else Is a Constant.

*"I always put 99 percent of effort into creative formats, creative angles, creative concepts, and creative volume."*

Targeting is commoditized. Broad works. Advantage+ works. The algorithm is smarter than your interest stacking. Stop overthinking audiences.

The thing that determines whether your campaign prints money or burns it is **the creative**. Full stop.

*"The biggest impact we can make at the account level is the creative."*

**The Höski Creative System:**

**Volume:** You need enough creative in the pipeline to test continuously. Minimum 4-5 new creatives per test cycle. Not 1\. Not 2\. Four to five.

**Diversity:** Every test batch must include different *formats* (static, video, carousel, UGC, founder-led) and different *angles* (problem-solution, testimonial, educational, urgency, lifestyle). If all 5 creatives look the same, you learned nothing.

**Testing Protocol:**

- Each creative gets $100-150 budget  
- Run for 3 days minimum (72 hours of data)  
- Measure: outbound CTR, hook rate (3-second video view %), cost per result  
- Kill anything below threshold. Scale winners.  
- Move winners to Evergreen campaign

**The Creative Hierarchy:**

```
1. Angle (what you're saying)
2. Hook (first 3 seconds / first line)
3. Format (video, static, carousel, UGC)
4. Offer (discount, bundle, free shipping)
5. Targeting (broad, lookalike, interest)
```

Most media buyers obsess over \#5. The money is in \#1 and \#2.

*"The moment we start doing something else like customer testimonial, there will be another 2 million or 3 million type of people that will identify with that."*

**Creative Angles That Work (from our data):**

| Angle | When to Use | Example |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Problem-Solution | Product solves a clear pain point | "Tired of \[problem\]? Here's what changed." |
| Social Proof / Testimonial | Product has real reviews and results | Customer video, screenshot of review |
| Founder Story | Brand has a compelling origin | Founder on camera, authentic, unpolished |
| Educational | Complex product or new category | "3 things you didn't know about \[product\]" |
| Urgency / Scarcity | Sale event or limited inventory | Countdown, "only X left", seasonal push |
| Lifestyle / Aspiration | Premium or fashion brand | Product in context, aspirational setting |
| UGC / Organic Feel | DTC ecom, younger demographic | Shot on phone, no branding, native to feed |

### Law 3: Trust the Blended Number, Not the Platform

*"MER is basically the abbreviation for media efficiency ratio. Total sales divided by total amount of ad spend."*

Platform ROAS lies. Meta takes credit for organic sales. Google takes credit for brand searches. Attribution windows overlap. Everyone's double-counting.

**MER (Media Efficiency Ratio) is the truth.**

```
MER = Total Revenue / Total Ad Spend
```

This is the number that tells you if your marketing is actually working. Not Meta ROAS. Not Google ROAS. The blended number.

**How to use both:**

- **Platform ROAS** → for optimization decisions *within* a platform (which campaign to scale, which to kill)  
- **MER** → for business decisions (should we spend more total? Are we profitable? Is growth real?)  
- **Non-direct last click** → in GA4, for directional channel comparison

**The Dashboard Every Client Gets:**

| Metric | Source | Purpose |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Breakeven CPA | Unit economics calc | The ceiling |
| Actual CPA | Platform | Are we hitting target? |
| MER | Shopify \+ all spend | Is the business growing? |
| New Customer % | Shopify / Triple Whale | Are we acquiring or just retargeting? |
| AOV | Shopify | Is the funnel working? |
| Email Revenue % | Klaviyo | Is retention carrying its weight? |

If MER is healthy but platform ROAS looks bad, you're fine. If platform ROAS looks great but MER is declining, you have a problem.

### Law 4: The Calendar Is the Strategy

*"You need to align the growth of the brand with certain moments in the marketing calendar."*

Reactive marketing is amateur marketing. If you're scrambling to build a Black Friday campaign in November, you already lost.

**The Höski Annual Calendar System:**

Every client gets a 12-month marketing calendar built in Month 1\. It maps:

- **Tier 1 Events** (must-win): BFCM, Q5 (Dec 26 \- Jan 7), Valentine's, Mother's Day  
- **Tier 2 Events** (brand-specific): industry dates, product launches, seasonal peaks  
- **Tier 3 Events** (opportunistic): cultural moments, trending topics, micro-holidays

**The 4-Week Prep Rule:** Every Tier 1 event gets a 4-week runway:

- **Week \-4:** Creative production begins. Briefs to creators.  
- **Week \-3:** Email sequences drafted. Landing pages built.  
- **Week \-2:** Creatives in review. Ad campaigns structured. Audiences built.  
- **Week \-1:** Everything scheduled. Test runs live. Final QA.  
- **Week 0:** Launch. Monitor hourly for first 48h.

*"We're going to prepare way earlier just to make sure that we don't overwhelm anybody with too much work right before that month."*

**Q5 is the hidden goldmine.** The week between Christmas and New Year's. CPMs crater (everyone else paused campaigns), but consumers are home with gift cards and money to spend. We never pause. We scale.

**BFCM Protocol:**

1. Pre-sale teaser emails start 2 weeks before  
2. VIP / email list gets early access (24-48 hours before public)  
3. Sale ads scheduled to go live at 5 AM EST on Black Friday  
4. Evergreen winners get sale overlays (banner at top, discount code)  
5. Retargeting ramps to 2x normal budget  
6. Email cadence: every other day through the full week  
7. Post-BFCM: "last chance" sequence for 48 hours after Cyber Monday

### Law 5: The Funnel Is the Product

*"You have to hold their hand and walk them through the entire customer journey."*

The ad is not the funnel. The ad is the door. The funnel is everything that happens after the click.

**The Full Funnel Architecture:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  TOP OF FUNNEL (Prospecting)                │
│  Goal: Awareness + qualified traffic         │
│  Campaigns: Broad, DCT, whitelisted ads     │
│  Budget: 60-70% of total spend              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  MIDDLE (Retargeting)                       │
│  Goal: Convert warm traffic                 │
│  Campaigns: DPA, social proof, testimonials │
│  Budget: 20-25% of total spend             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  BOTTOM (Retention)                         │
│  Goal: Repeat purchase + LTV               │
│  Channels: Email, SMS, loyalty              │
│  Budget: 10-15% of total spend             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Prospecting** is where you test creative and find new customers. This is where the creative system (Law 2\) lives.

**Retargeting** is where you close. DPA (Dynamic Product Ads) for product-specific retargeting. Social proof ads for trust-building. Short windows: 7-14 day retargeting audiences perform best.

**Retention** is where profit lives. First-order profitability is the goal from ads. Second, third, fourth orders are where the real margin comes from. Email and SMS do this work.

*"First order profitability is the North Star. We need to make sure that every customer we drive into the funnel is profitable somehow in the back end for the brand."*

**The Landing Page Rule:** Never send cold traffic to a product page that isn't optimized. Every landing page must have:

- Clear hero image / video above the fold  
- One primary CTA (not three)  
- Social proof within first scroll (reviews, logos, press)  
- Trust signals (shipping, returns, guarantees)  
- Mobile-first design (70%+ of traffic is mobile)

*"We can add reviews, social proof, and a clearer call-to-action button."*

## Part 3: The Operating System

The Five Laws are *what* we do. The Operating System is *how* we do it, day by day, week by week.

### The Weekly Rhythm

Every pod, every account, every week:

| Day | Activity |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Monday** | Review weekend performance. Kill underperformers. Scale winners. Plan creative needs for the week. |
| **Tuesday-Wednesday** | Creative production. New assets in pipeline. Brief creators. |
| **Thursday** | Launch new test batch (4-5 creatives). Set up campaigns. |
| **Friday** | Weekly performance review. Update hypothesis tracker. Update marketing calendar. Flag red flags. |

### The Hypothesis Tracker

*"That one spreadsheet gives me a lot of perspective."*

Every test has a hypothesis. Every result gets recorded. This is how you compound knowledge instead of testing randomly.

| Week | Hypothesis | Test | Result | Learning | Next Action |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| W1 | UGC outperforms studio for this audience | 3 UGC vs 2 studio creatives | UGC 2.1x CTR, 40% lower CPA | This audience responds to authenticity | Scale top UGC. Brief 3 more UGC creators. |
| W2 | Problem-solution hook beats lifestyle | 2 problem-solution vs 2 lifestyle | Problem-solution 30% lower CPA | Pain point resonance is strong | Double down. Test different pain points. |

**No test without a hypothesis. No result without a learning. No learning without a next action.**

### The Campaign Structure

**Meta Account Structure (Standard):**

```
📁 PROSPECTING
   ├── 🧪 Testing (CBO, $100-150/creative, broad)
   ├── 🏆 Evergreen (winning creatives, scaled budget)
   └── 💰 Cost Cap (target CPA, inflated budget)

📁 RETARGETING
   ├── 🛒 DPA (7-14 day window, product catalog)
   └── 🎯 Custom Audiences (site visitors, add-to-cart, email list)

📁 RETENTION
   └── 📧 Email/SMS list suppression from prospecting
```

**Google Ads Structure (Standard):**

```
📁 BRAND (branded search terms)
📁 NON-BRAND SEARCH (high-intent keywords)
📁 SHOPPING (standard + Performance Max)
📁 DISPLAY/YOUTUBE (retargeting only, unless testing)
```

**Budget Allocation Rule of Thumb:**

- 60-70% Meta (prospecting \+ retargeting)  
- 20-30% Google (search \+ shopping)  
- 10% testing / experimental channels

Adjust based on vertical. Local services skew Google. DTC ecom skews Meta. B2B skews Google \+ LinkedIn.

### The Whitelisting Play

*"Have you ever heard about this third-party whitelisting approach?"*

Running ads from a brand handle screams "ad." Running the same ad from a founder's personal handle or an influencer's handle feels native. Authentic. Real.

**When to use whitelisting:**

- DTC brands with a strong founder story  
- Products that benefit from personal endorsement  
- UGC-heavy accounts where creator content outperforms branded  
- Any time you're seeing ad fatigue on branded content

**How it works:**

1. Creator or founder grants ad permissions to brand's ad account  
2. Ads run under the creator's handle (their name, their profile pic)  
3. Creative looks like an organic post, not an ad  
4. Engagement goes to the creator's post (social proof compounds)

This is one of the highest-leverage plays in DTC media buying. Use it.

## Part 4: The Client Lifecycle

### Phase 1: Onboarding (Week 1-2)

The first two weeks determine whether a client succeeds or churns. Get this right.

**Week 1 Checklist:**

- [ ] Unit economics calculator completed  
- [ ] Ad account access granted and audited  
- [ ] Pixel / CAPI verified  
- [ ] Google Analytics access confirmed  
- [ ] Shopify / CMS access confirmed  
- [ ] Klaviyo / email platform access confirmed  
- [ ] Competitor audit (3-5 competitors, check ad libraries)  
- [ ] 12-month marketing calendar drafted  
- [ ] First creative brief sent to team  
- [ ] Onboarding call recorded and notes filed

**Week 2 Checklist:**

- [ ] First campaign structure built  
- [ ] First batch of creatives in production  
- [ ] Email flows audited (welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase)  
- [ ] Landing page audit complete  
- [ ] First test batch ready to launch  
- [ ] Client expectations documented (targets, reporting cadence, communication preferences)

*"We've created our onboarding process. It's the foundation."*

### Phase 2: Testing (Month 1-2)

This is the learning phase. You're spending money to learn, not to scale.

**Goals:**

- Find 2-3 winning creative angles  
- Establish baseline CPA and ROAS  
- Identify best-performing audiences (usually broad)  
- Test offer structures (discounts, bundles, free shipping)  
- Build retargeting audiences from initial traffic

**Rules:**

- Don't scale prematurely. $100-150/creative test budget. 72-hour minimum.  
- Kill fast. If CPA is 3x target after $150 spend, it's dead.  
- Document everything in the hypothesis tracker.  
- Communicate results to client weekly. Set expectations: month 1 is data, not profit.

### Phase 3: Scaling (Month 3+)

You've found what works. Now push it.

**Scaling Rules:**

- Increase budget 20-30% every 3-5 days on winning campaigns (not overnight)  
- Monitor CPA daily during scaling. If CPA spikes \>30% above target for 3+ days, pull back.  
- Continue feeding new creative into the testing campaign. Winners move to Evergreen.  
- Scale email/SMS in parallel. Retention grows profit while ads grow revenue.

*"When you have this kind of blended approach, that's the right way of growing a brand."*

**Cost Cap Strategy (for stable scaling):**

1. Set a CPA or ROAS target on the campaign  
2. Give it 3-5x the normal daily budget  
3. The campaign will only spend when it can acquire at your target  
4. This prevents runaway spend during scaling

*"We're going to set up a certain ROAS target or CPA target, and then give the campaign an inflated budget."*

### Phase 4: Optimization (Ongoing)

The work never stops. Accounts that plateau are accounts that stopped testing.

**Monthly Optimization Checklist:**

- [ ] Refresh creative (new batch every 2-3 weeks minimum)  
- [ ] Review and update marketing calendar  
- [ ] Audit email flows (are they still performing?)  
- [ ] Check competitor ad libraries (what are they running?)  
- [ ] Review landing page performance (bounce rate, conversion rate)  
- [ ] Update hypothesis tracker  
- [ ] Run customer cohort analysis (are repeat purchase rates improving?)  
- [ ] Review MER trend (up, down, flat?)

**Red Flags That Demand Immediate Action:**

| Red Flag | What It Means | What to Do |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| CPA rising 3 weeks in a row | Creative fatigue or market shift | New creative batch. Test new angles. Check competitors. |
| MER declining while platform ROAS is stable | Attribution is lying. You're retargeting, not acquiring. | Check new customer %. Shift budget to prospecting. |
| AOV dropping | Customers buying cheaper items or less per order | Review product mix. Test bundles. Check offer structure. |
| Email open rates below 20% | List health issues or bad subject lines | Clean list. Segment. A/B test subject lines. |
| Client asking "what are you doing?" | Communication gap | Proactive reporting. Don't wait for them to ask. |

## Part 5: The Verticals

The Five Laws apply everywhere. But execution varies by vertical.

### E-Commerce (DTC)

**Primary lever:** Creative volume \+ offer testing **Key metric:** First-order profitability \+ 90-day LTV **Campaign structure:** Full funnel (prospecting, retargeting, retention) **Email is critical:** Welcome flow, abandoned cart, post-purchase, winback, VIP **Typical budget split:** 70% Meta, 25% Google Shopping, 5% testing

*"Our job is to identify the two or three top selling products with the highest volume of sales. And you need to focus on those as much as possible."*

### Local Services (Dental, Home Services, Medical)

**Primary lever:** Google Search \+ lead quality **Key metric:** Cost per qualified lead (not just cost per lead) **Campaign structure:** Search-heavy, local targeting, call tracking **Typical budget split:** 70% Google, 20% Meta (awareness \+ retargeting), 10% LSA

### B2B / Lead Gen

**Primary lever:** Targeting precision \+ landing page conversion **Key metric:** Cost per qualified meeting booked **Campaign structure:** LinkedIn for targeting, Meta for retargeting, Google for intent **Typical budget split:** 40% LinkedIn, 30% Google, 30% Meta retargeting

## Part 6: The Mindset

This section isn't about tactics. It's about how you think.

*"The language that you use is your reality."*

### Think Like an Owner, Not an Operator

You are not a button-pusher. You are managing someone's money to grow their business. Every dollar you spend is a dollar they earned. Treat it that way.

When you optimize a campaign, you're not "adjusting bids." You're deciding whether this business grows or shrinks this month. Act accordingly.

### Data Over Feelings

*"So the data is what is driving our decision-making. When we need to kill campaigns, when we need to scale them up, when we need to scale them down."*

You don't "feel like" a campaign is working. You *know* it is because the numbers say so. If you can't point to the data that supports your decision, you don't have a decision. You have a guess.

### Test, Don't Debate

Don't argue about whether a hook will work. Test it. Don't debate whether UGC beats studio. Run both. The data will tell you. Opinions are free. Data costs $150 and 72 hours.

### Speed Wins

*"Things are changing very fast. And this is not just like... Hey, I'm coming in, I'm doing my job, and I'm leaving, because truthfully, if we all did that, this organization wouldn't exist in two years."*

The agency that tests 20 creatives a month beats the agency that tests 5\. The team that launches on Monday beats the team that launches on Friday. Speed is a competitive advantage. Don't be reckless, but don't be slow.

### Message Testing Is the Highest Leverage Activity

*"The biggest priority all the time should be message testing."*

The offer, the angle, the hook, the words you use. This is where the money is. You can have perfect campaign structure and optimal targeting, and if the message is wrong, it all fails. Message testing is not something you do after the campaign is set up. It IS the campaign.

### Five Stages of Awareness

*"There will always be five types of customers. Unaware, problem aware, solution aware, product aware, most aware."*

Every piece of creative you make should target a specific stage:

| Stage | What They Know | What Your Ad Does |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Unaware** | Nothing about the problem | Educate. Create the problem. |
| **Problem Aware** | They have a problem | Agitate the problem. Introduce a solution exists. |
| **Solution Aware** | Solutions exist | Position your product as the best solution. |
| **Product Aware** | Your product exists | Differentiate. Social proof. Offer. |
| **Most Aware** | Ready to buy | Close. Urgency. Best deal. |

Cold prospecting campaigns should target stages 1-3. Retargeting should target 4-5. Most agencies only run stage 4-5 creative and wonder why prospecting doesn't work.
