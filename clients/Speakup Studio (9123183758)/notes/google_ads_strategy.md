## 1B. Unit Economics — CPL Target Validation

Before presenting this strategy to Charles, confirm the four inputs below. Estimates are used until actual numbers are provided.

| Input | Estimated | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Average client value (first 3 months) | $400 CAD | $167 entry session + typical upsell to package |
| Lead-to-close rate | 30% | Industry baseline for consultation-gated creative studios |
| Show rate (booked call → actually shows) | 80% | High — automated calendar booking reduces no-shows |
| Gross margin | ~60% | Studio-based business; low COGS vs. agency |

**CPL Ceiling Math:**
```
Max CPL = Avg Client Value × Close Rate × Show Rate
Max CPL = $400 × 0.30 × 0.80 = $96 CAD

Target CPL = Max CPL × 0.75 = ~$72 CAD
```

**Conclusion:** The $50-100 CAD CPL target in Section 10 is defensible at conservative estimates. If average client value is $600+ (package buyers upselling to recurring sessions), the CPL ceiling rises to $144 CAD and the current budget is even more efficient.

**Questions to ask Charles before launch:**
1. "What percentage of discovery calls turn into paying clients?" (close rate)
2. "What does the average new client spend in their first 1-3 months?" (avg client value)

Do not change the CPL target without running the math above with actual numbers from Charles.

## 2. Market & Competitive Landscape

### Montreal Podcast/Video Studio Market
The Montreal studio market is fragmented across rental-focused and production-focused providers. Most competitors compete on **price and equipment**, not **results and authority**. Speak Up Studio's angle of *"Build Authority"* is a significant differentiator.

### Key Competitors

| Competitor | Model | Starting Price | Key Weakness vs Speak Up |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OnPod Studios** (onpod.io/montreal) | Automated video podcast, self-service | Hourly packages | Fully automated, no direction/coaching; Laval location is closest proximity threat |
| **Archetype MTL** (archetypemtl.com) | Operator-supported podcast/video studio | Not published | No bilingual mention; no entrepreneur-first personal brand angle |
| **Blabla Studio** (blablastudio.ca) | Self-service turnkey rental | ~$150/session | No direction/coaching; pure rental; no bilingual mention |
| **Virage Sonore** (virage-sonore.com) | Audio/video studio rental | ~$89-$104/hr | Audio-first focus; less video/personal brand angle |
| **Expaz Studios** | Self-service studio | ~$94.99/hr | No guidance; DIY audience |
| **Porte Parole** | Audio podcast studio | ~$90/hr | Audio only, no video, no personal brand angle |
| **Great Things Studios** | Full-service production | ~$200+/hr | Much more expensive; no clear entrepreneur focus |
| **Giggster listings** | Marketplace rentals | $60-$245/hr | No brand identity; generic marketplace |

### Competitive Gap Spot
**There is NO dominant player in Montreal/Laval targeting professionals and entrepreneurs specifically for LinkedIn authority content + bilingual (EN/FR) podcast + video packages.** This is Speak Up Studio's lane. Most competitors are fighting over studio rental searchers; Speak Up should own the "content strategy + production for professionals" angle.

### Key Competitor Weaknesses
1. None of the major competitors have a **"one session = weeks of content"** model as their primary pitch
2. Laval has only **OnPod** as a studio competitor — and their model is fully automated (no human direction, no coaching, no strategy). Speakup's directed model wins on relevance for any professional who wants guidance, not just a room.
3. Few if any run **bilingual Google Ads** — English AND French funnels. The French-language personal brand / LinkedIn angle is completely unoccupied in paid search.
4. No competitor is clearly targeting **LinkedIn creators, coaches, or consultants** as a primary segment
5. Speakup's **$167 price point** is genuinely differentiated — above Giggster/commodity ($60-$90/hr) but far below full-service agency ($3k-$10k). It's the professional entry point no one else clearly occupies.
