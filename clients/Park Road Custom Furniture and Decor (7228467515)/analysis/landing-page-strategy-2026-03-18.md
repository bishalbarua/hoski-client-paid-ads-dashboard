# Landing Page Strategy — Park Road Custom Furniture
**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Campaigns:** Custom Furniture + Cottage Furniture
**Offer:** $750 off first custom order of $5,000+
**Goal:** Lead generation — form submissions + phone calls

---

## Why Two Pages Are Needed

Same offer ($750 off $5k+) runs across both campaigns. But message match between ad and landing page directly affects:
- **Quality Score** — Google rewards relevance between keyword → ad → landing page
- **Conversion rate** — visitors bounce when the page doesn't reflect what they searched for
- **Cost per lead** — higher QS = lower CPC = cheaper leads

A person searching "furnish my Muskoka cottage" landing on a page about a London showroom and custom builds feels like the wrong place. They leave. The page copy needs to mirror the search intent.

---

## Page 1: Custom Furniture

**Serves:** Custom Furniture campaign (London, Ontario geo)
**Buyer intent:** Someone actively looking for custom/built-to-order furniture, likely local to London or Southwestern Ontario

### Copy Framework

| Element | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Headline** | Custom Furniture, Built to Order — $750 Off $5,000+ |
| **Sub-headline** | Visit our London, Ontario showroom. Every piece built to your specs, in solid Canadian wood. |
| **Body (2–3 lines)** | Not mass-produced. Not flat-pack. Every piece Park Road builds is designed with you and built by hand in London, Ontario. Choose your wood, your finish, your dimensions — we handle the rest. |
| **Offer callout** | Get $750 off your first custom order of $5,000 or more. Fill out the form to claim your discount and book a free consultation. |
| **CTA button** | Get a Free Quote |
| **Phone** | Prominent click-to-call, above the fold on mobile |
| **Trust line** | Handcrafted in London, Ontario since 2012 |
| **Social proof** | Photos of completed pieces — dining rooms, living rooms, bedrooms |

### Form Fields
- Name
- Phone
- Email
- What are you looking for? (short text or dropdown: Dining Room / Living Room / Bedroom / Other)

## What Stays the Same on Both Pages

- Offer amount and threshold ($750 off $5,000+)
- Form structure (same 4 fields)
- Phone number (same click-to-call)
- Page layout and structure
- Park Road branding

## What Changes Between Pages

| Element | Custom Furniture Page | Cottage Furniture Page |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | Custom Furniture, Built to Order | Furnish Your Cottage |
| Sub-headline | London, Ontario showroom | Muskoka, Georgian Bay |
| Body copy angle | Built-to-spec, showroom experience | Durability, cottage life, heirloom quality |
| CTA button text | Get a Free Quote | Claim Your Cottage Discount |
| Hero image | Showroom / dining room / living room | Cottage interior / rustic farmhouse setting |
| Trust line | Handcrafted in London, Ontario since 2012 | Built to outlast the cottage season |

---

## Implementation Notes

- Both pages can be built from the same template — swap headline, body copy, hero image, CTA text
- Estimated build time: under 1 hour per page using any page builder (Elementor, Divi, etc.)
- No nav menu on either page — keep visitors focused on the form
- No links that take visitors away from the page
- A/B test CTA button copy after 50+ conversions: "Get a Free Quote" vs. "Claim Your $750 Discount"
