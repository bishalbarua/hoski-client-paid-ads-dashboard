Park Road Custom Furniture: Offer Strategy Update

Been doing competitive research on what other custom furniture businesses are running for promotions. Here's the summary:

What competitors are doing
Local London competitors (This Oak House, Rustix Studio, Mennonite Crafted) — no discounts at all. They compete purely on craft and story.

National players are more aggressive:
• Naked Furniture — 15% + extra 10% stacked, seasonal
• Acre Made — $500 off first order of $5k+, tied to email signup
• Woodcraft — 30% storewide "March Madness" with a hard deadline
The pattern: discount + minimum order threshold + urgency deadline = the winning formula among those converting online.

My recommendation for Park Road
Instead of 15% off $10k+ (threshold too high, screens out most buyers):

→ "$750 off your first custom order of $5,000 or more."

Why: dollar amounts feel bigger than percentages, $5k qualifies real buyers without being out of reach, and "first order" creates urgency to act now.

What the Data Actually Tells Us
London, Ontario is a small market. Custom furniture terms pull 10–30 searches/month locally. This is normal for a niche, high-ticket product — but it has implications:
Total addressable volume across all ad groups: ~4,500/mo
At 8–12% CTR: ~360–540 clicks/month
At ~$1.50 avg CPC: $500–$800/month captures most of the relevant market

The good news: CPCs are very cheap. $0.67–$2.59 for high-intent terms. For a $5k–$15k ticket, even a $50–$80 CPL is excellent economics.

London alone won't generate enough volume to optimize quickly. We would need to expand to Southwestern Ontario: Kitchener, Waterloo, Windsor, Guelph, and Woodstock. Park Road delivers regionally, so this is justified and won't send unqualified traffic.

Campaign setup
• Goal: Lead generation (form submissions + phone calls): purchase happens in-store
• No e-commerce needed
• We need a dedicated landing page for this offer (not the homepage): simple, one job: headline, 4-field form, phone number, a few product photos
• Two conversion actions to track: form fills + calls (CallRail)

Action steps:
 Confirm offer details with the client (amount, threshold, expiry date)
 Build a landing page with offer messaging
 Set up CallRail and a call tracking number for the page 
 Launch Search campaign targeting custom furniture keywords in London, Southwestern, ON

Park Road Custom Furniture: Cottage Furniture Research
 
Did a full competitor + keyword deep dive on the cottage furniture opportunity. Here's everything we need to know.

What competitors are doing
Almost nothing online. Every independent Ontario cottage furniture maker we looked up had a dead, broken, or non-existent website. The paid search landscape for "cottage furniture ontario" and "muskoka furniture" is dominated entirely by big-box retailers (Wayfair, Leon's, IKEA) running generic, non-relevant ads.

Three named competitors do have brand search volume worth watching: 
• Simply Cottage (Bracebridge): 480 searches/month for their brand 
• Hemlock Cottage Furniture: 70/mo 
• Pioneer Furniture Muskoka: 30/mo

None of them appear to be running active Google Ads. No specialist is bidding on cottage furniture terms in Ontario right now.

Bottom line: this space is wide open. Park Road can own it.

Key findings from keyword research
Pulled live data from Google Keyword Planner (Ontario + Canada-wide). A few things stood out:

Muskoka furniture — 880 searches/month, nearly no competition in paid search. "Muskoka" is the highest-value identity signal in Ontario cottage culture and nobody is using it in custom furniture ads.
Farmhouse dining table — 880/mo per variation, 15+ query forms. Collectively 3,000–4,000 impressions/month. This is the volume engine of the campaign.
Cottage sofa/couch/chairs/coffee table — solid room-specific intent, 40–260/mo each, very cheap CPCs ($0.40–$2.50)
Cottage bedroom/beds for cottages — 110/mo, low CPCs, good fit if Park Road builds bedroom pieces
 
Total addressable search volume across Ontario: 8,000–12,000 searches/month — 2-3x more than the London-only custom furniture campaign because this one targets province-wide.

What nobody is saying (our opportunity)
Three angles that are completely unoccupied by any competitor:
"Muskoka/cottage country" by name: nobody is saying "furnish your Muskoka cottage." That word signals affluence and Ontario cottage culture, and zero advertisers are using it for custom furniture.
Cottage-country durability: solid wood for humidity, sunscreen, and winter storage. A real functional reason imports fall short. Nobody mentions this.
Heirloom framing: "furniture your grandkids will fight over." Big-box buyers replace furniture every 5 years. Custom solid wood is a generational purchase. Nobody is saying this.

Recommendation
Run this as a second campaign alongside the custom furniture campaign, not a replacement. Same offer ($750 off $5k+),
Separate landing page with only different copies and imagery tuned to cottage/Muskoka lifestyle.
 
Action steps
 Confirm Park Road's delivery coverage — do they deliver to Muskoka/Georgian Bay/cottage country?
 Confirm if they make bunk beds and custom log beds (keywords exist for these)
 Create a separate landing page with different copies and cottage/Muskoka imagery (can share the $750 off offer)
 Phase 2: build competitor campaigns targeting Simply Cottage, Hemlock Cottage, and Pioneer Furniture brand searches
