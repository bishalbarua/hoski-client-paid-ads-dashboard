## IMAGE DIRECTION

Images are doing half the message match work on this page. A visitor who searched "muskoka furniture" or "cottage dining table" and lands on a page with city showroom photos will bounce before reading a word of copy. Every image on this page must feel like cottage country, not a London showroom.

### The Core Visual Difference from the Custom Furniture Page

| | Custom Furniture Page | Cottage Furniture Page |
|---|---|---|
| Hero background | Modern interior, city home, warm showroom | Cottage interior: natural light, exposed wood beams or ceiling, rustic dining or living room |
| Palette | Warm neutrals, contemporary finishes | Warm wood tones, deep greens, natural stone, linen |
| Setting | Urban home, London Ontario | Cottage, lake house, farmhouse, Georgian Bay or Muskoka-style interior |
| Furniture aesthetic | Clean lines, custom but contemporary | Rustic, farmhouse, live edge, solid plank, log-inspired |
| People (if any) | Homeowner meeting a designer | Family around a farmhouse dining table, or a quiet cottage living room scene |

### Source Priority
1. **Park Road's own portfolio** — always first for the portfolio section. Any rustic, farmhouse, live edge, or cottage-aesthetic pieces they've built.
2. **AI-generated images** — approved for hero, lifestyle, consultation, and accent sections. Prompts provided per section below.
3. **Unsplash (free)** — fallback if AI output doesn't meet the quality bar. Search terms listed.
4. **Creative director** — UX designer to escalate specific image needs as required.

> **Note for UX designer:** The hero image on this page is the highest-stakes creative decision in the whole project. A Muskoka/cottage interior that makes the visitor feel "I want that at my cottage" does message match work before they read a single word. Use the AI prompts below as a starting point and iterate with the creative director if needed.

### AI Image Prompts

**Hero background (most important image on the page):**
> Photorealistic interior photography of a rustic Ontario cottage dining room. Large solid wood farmhouse dining table, plank top with visible grain, seats 8–10. Warm afternoon light through wide windows overlooking trees or a lake. Exposed wood ceiling beams. Simple linen curtains. Farmhouse-style chairs with natural wood frames. A bowl of fruit or simple centerpiece on the table. Warm amber and green tones. No people. Wide angle, full room. Horizontal 16:9 crop. Feels like Muskoka or Georgian Bay cottage life.

**Hero background (alternative — living room version):**
> Photorealistic interior photography of a cozy Ontario cottage living room. Solid wood coffee table with visible grain, rustic sofa with linen upholstery, warm natural light from windows showing forest or lake outside. Exposed ceiling beams. Woven rug, a few books stacked on the coffee table. No TV. Warm honey and green tones. No people. Wide angle, 16:9 crop. Feels lived-in, not staged.

**Consultation section:**
> Photorealistic close-up of wood stain samples and finish swatches fanned out on a rough-hewn barnboard or reclaimed wood table surface. Warm natural light from the left. Samples range from light ash to deep walnut. A hand-drawn floor sketch or rough pencil notes visible at the edge. Rustic, warm, artisan atmosphere. Shallow depth of field. No text in the image.

**Footer CTA accent (optional):**
> Extreme close-up photorealistic photo of the corner joint of a solid wood farmhouse dining table. Mortise and tenon joinery visible. Deep walnut or oak grain. Warm side lighting. Feels handcrafted and built to last. No people, no clutter. Horizontal crop.

### Portfolio Section — Detailed Image Direction

The portfolio section must feel visually different from the Custom Furniture page. The same Park Road pieces can appear, but selection and order should prioritize the cottage/rustic aesthetic.

**Our Dining Room Collection (shown first — highest keyword volume)**
- Lead with the most rustic/farmhouse dining table Park Road has built
- Long plank table, visible wood grain, farmhouse-style chairs, warm light
- Feature a live edge dining table if they have one
- Avoid showing contemporary or modern dining rooms in the first 2 photos

**Our Bedroom Collection (shown second)**
- Solid wood plank headboard, log-inspired or rustic post bed frames
- If they have bunk beds, include them — "cottage bunk beds" is a live search term
- Avoid white lacquer or ultra-modern bedroom pieces in the first 2 photos

**Our Living Room Collection**
- Rustic coffee tables, cottage sofas, wood accent pieces
- Warm textiles, natural light, cozy feel — not sleek contemporary

**Our Office, Bar, Lighting/Decor Collections**
- Lower priority for this campaign audience
- If photos are mostly modern or urban, consider placing these last or reducing their prominence

### If Park Road Doesn't Have Cottage-Specific Portfolio Photos
- **Option A:** Use their warmest/most rustic pieces regardless of setting — a solid farmhouse dining table reads as farmhouse wherever it was photographed.
- **Option B:** Ask Park Road if any pieces are installed at cottage or Muskoka locations. One authentic cottage install photo outperforms 10 studio shots.
- **Option C:** Escalate to creative director for AI-generated "inspiration" room scenes to supplement. Label clearly as "Custom designs inspired by your space" if not actual Park Road work.

### Tone and Style Guide for All Images (Cottage Furniture Page)
- **Mood:** Warm, lived-in, built to last. Feels like real cottage country — not a hotel lobby or a design magazine.
- **Color palette:** Honey wood tones, deep forest greens, lake blues, warm whites and linens, natural stone accents.
- **Light:** Natural light, golden hour where possible. Avoid flash photography or harsh studio lighting.
- **Scale:** Show furniture at human scale — a table that seats 8, a bed with rumpled linen, a coffee table with a book on it. Makes it feel real, not a product shot.
- **Avoid:** White-background product photography, ultra-contemporary minimalist settings, anything that looks like a condo showroom, AI-generated rooms that look too perfect.

## USER JOURNEY FLOW

### Why the Flow Was Changed

Same reason as the Custom Furniture page. Previous flow (form → calendar → thank you) means abandoned calendar bookings are invisible in Google Ads. A lead who fills the form is a conversion — that should fire immediately at TY1 regardless of whether they book the calendar.

### New Flow

```
Landing Page
    ↓
User fills out 4-field form
    ↓
Thank You Page 1  ← PRIMARY conversion fires here (form fill)
    ↓ (optional next step — user's choice)
User clicks "Book Your Consultation" CTA
    ↓
Calendar booking page (GHL calendar or Calendly embed)
    ↓
Thank You Page 2  ← SECONDARY conversion fires here (consultation booked)
```

### Conversion Tracking Setup in Google Ads

| Conversion Action | Trigger | Designation | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|---|
| Form Submission | User lands on `/cottage-furniture/thank-you` | **Primary** | Lead captured — the main KPI |
| Consultation Booked | User lands on `/cottage-furniture/consultation-booked` | **Secondary** | Lead quality — what % of form fills actually book |

Both are URL-based conversions. No advanced tag setup needed — the base Google Tag on the funnel handles it.

**Note:** Keep the Custom Furniture and Cottage Furniture conversion actions separate in Google Ads. This lets you compare lead volume and CPL between the two campaigns cleanly. Name them: `CF - Form Submission`, `CF - Consultation Booked`, `Cottage - Form Submission`, `Cottage - Consultation Booked`.
