### Tone and Style Guide for All Images (Custom Furniture Page)
- **Mood:** Warm, skilled, considered. Feels like a real craftsperson's work, not a factory or a catalogue.
- **Color palette:** Wood tones, warm whites, soft grays. Avoid anything that looks clinical or flat-pack.
- **People:** If people appear, they should look like real homeowners or designers — not stock model poses.
- **Avoid:** White-background product photography, overly styled "magazine" shots that look unattainable, anything that suggests mass production.

## MOBILE OPTIMIZATION NOTES (Read Before Building)

The majority of paid traffic will be on mobile. These rules apply throughout the build:

- Sticky header: phone number (tap-to-call) + CTA button. Visible at ALL scroll depths.
- Hero CTA button: full width on mobile, minimum 52px height. Do not let it get cut off.
- Form: 4 fields only (no budget dropdown, no meeting preference). Fewer fields = more submissions on cold paid traffic.
- Headline font size: 36–40px on mobile. The current page headline is readable — keep that sizing.
- Hero background image: use a dark overlay so white text stays legible on all phones.
- Countdown timer: large digits, centered, readable at a glance on a 375px screen.
- Pain point cards: stack vertically, full width. No horizontal scroll.
- Comparison table: on mobile, show Park Road column + one competitor column at a time, or convert to a stacked checklist. Full 4-column table does not render well on 375px.
- Portfolio images: single column grid on mobile. 2 images per row max.
- CTA buttons throughout: full width on mobile, not inline text links.
- Phone number everywhere it appears: tap-to-call href="tel:+1XXXXXXXXXX"
- No pop-ups or interstitials that block the form on mobile.

## USER JOURNEY FLOW

### Why the Flow Was Changed

The previous flow sent users from the form directly to the calendar page. This breaks conversion tracking because:
- If a user fills the form but abandons the calendar, no conversion is recorded in Google Ads — that lead is invisible to Smart Bidding
- The calendar tool may redirect to its own confirmation page on a different domain, making tracking unreliable
- Form fill and consultation booking are two distinct intent signals — they should be tracked separately

### New Flow

```
Landing Page
    ↓
User fills out 4-field form
    ↓
Thank You Page 1  ← PRIMARY conversion fires here (form fill)
    ↓ (optional next step — user's choice)
User clicks "Book Your Consultation" CTA
    ↓
Calendar booking page (GHL calendar or Calendly embed)
    ↓
Thank You Page 2  ← SECONDARY conversion fires here (consultation booked)
```

### Conversion Tracking Setup in Google Ads

| Conversion Action | Trigger | Designation | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|---|
| Form Submission | User lands on `/custom-furniture/thank-you` | **Primary** | Lead captured — the main KPI |
| Consultation Booked | User lands on `/custom-furniture/consultation-booked` | **Secondary** | Lead quality — what % of form fills actually book |

Set both as URL-based conversions in Google Ads (simple page visit trigger — no tag customization needed beyond the base Google Tag already on the funnel).
