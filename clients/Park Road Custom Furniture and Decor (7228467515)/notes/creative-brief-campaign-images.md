# Creative Brief — Campaign Images
**Client:** Park Road Custom Furniture and Decor
**Campaigns:** Custom Furniture | Cottage Furniture
**Date:** 2026-03-21
**Prepared by:** Bishal (Creative Director)
**Total images:** 10 (5 per campaign)

---

## Creative Director's Note

These images are not decoration. They are doing active message match work. A visitor arriving from a Google ad has already formed an expectation based on the headline they clicked. If the landing page image doesn't match that expectation in the first 2 seconds, they leave before reading a word of copy.

**The two campaigns must look and feel completely different from each other.** Custom Furniture = contemporary Canadian home, skilled craftsperson, city buyer. Cottage Furniture = Muskoka, Georgian Bay, rustic, lived-in, built to last generations. If someone sees an image from one page and it could have come from the other, the brief has failed.

**Image source priority (applies to all 10 images):**
1. **Check the client drive first** — before doing anything, review Park Road's existing photo assets on the shared drive. They may already have usable photos of their work: installed pieces, in-progress builds, workshop shots, customer installs. Do not skip this step.
2. **Request real footage from the client** — if the drive doesn't have what's needed for a specific slot, ask Park Road directly. Tell them exactly what you need (e.g. "a photo of your most rustic dining table" or "any photos of pieces installed at a cottage or farmhouse"). Real photos of their actual work always outperform AI.
3. **Client photos + AI enhancement** — if Park Road provides a photo with the right content but poor quality (bad lighting, wrong crop, cluttered background), use AI to enhance, relight, or extend it. A real piece of furniture in an AI-improved setting beats a fabricated one.
4. **AI-generated from scratch** — only after steps 1–3 have been exhausted. Use the prompts below as a starting point. Iterate until the output matches the brief. Do not substitute with a generic stock photo — escalate to Bishal instead.

**Technical specs for all images:**
- Format: JPG (high quality) or PNG if transparency needed
- Hero images: minimum 1920px wide, 16:9 crop
- Section images: minimum 1200px wide
- Detail/accent images: minimum 800px wide, any crop that suits the section
- No text, logos, or watermarks in any image
- No AI artifacts (unnatural fingers, distorted objects, broken reflections)

## CAMPAIGN 1: Custom Furniture

**Campaign feeling:** Skilled craftsmanship, contemporary home, London Ontario, solid Canadian wood. The buyer is a homeowner who wants something built right, built to fit, and built to last. Premium but approachable — not a design magazine, not a factory showroom.

**Color palette:** Warm wood tones, warm whites and soft grays, warm natural light. No cool tones. No bright white studio lighting.

---

### Image 1 — Hero Background

**Placement:** Full-width hero section background (behind the headline and form)
**Dimensions:** 1920 x 1080px minimum, 16:9 horizontal

**What this image must do:**
This is the first thing every visitor sees when they arrive from a Google ad. It needs to communicate three things simultaneously before they read a word: (1) this is a real craftsperson's work, (2) this is a contemporary home setting they can picture themselves in, (3) the furniture is the hero, not the room. A visitor who searched "custom dining table london ontario" should feel "that's exactly what I was looking for" before reading the headline.

**What to show:**
A beautifully crafted solid wood dining table in the center of a warm, contemporary Canadian home dining room. The table is the clear focal point — full plank top, visible natural wood grain, clean lines but clearly handmade. The room around it is simple: hardwood floors, neutral walls, natural light coming through large windows. A few simple place settings or a candle on the table — enough to show scale and feel lived-in, not a product shot. No people. Wide angle showing the full room.

**AI generation prompt:**
> Photorealistic interior photography. A beautifully crafted solid wood dining table in a warm, contemporary Canadian home dining room. Wide plank top with visible natural grain. Simply set — a few candles, clean neutral dishware, no clutter. Hardwood floors, soft white walls, large windows with warm afternoon natural light. No people. The table is clearly handmade and high quality. Wide angle showing the full room. Warm color grading — amber and cream tones. 16:9 horizontal crop. Camera at mid height, eye level with the table. Feels like a real home, not a showroom or magazine set.

**What to avoid:**
- Ultra-modern minimalist aesthetic (Scandinavian white box)
- Rustic or farmhouse style (that's Campaign 2)
- Dark or dramatic lighting
- Busy room with too many decorative objects
- Any AI artifacts on the table surface or grain

### Image 2 — Consultation / Process Section

**Placement:** Section 9 (Consultation Details section) — lifestyle photo beside or behind the consultation copy
**Dimensions:** 1200 x 800px minimum, landscape or portrait depending on GHL layout

**What this image must do:**
Visualize the consultation process. The visitor has read the headline and form offer. Now they're learning about the next step: a designer comes to their home. This image should make that process feel real, warm, and low-pressure. It should communicate "a craftsperson who cares about getting it right."

**What to show:**
A close-up of wood stain and finish samples fanned out on a raw wood table surface. Warm natural light from the side. Samples range from light maple/ash to deep walnut — showing the range of choice. A pencil and notepad visible at the edge, suggesting a working consultation. No people's faces, no corporate feel. Feels like a real artisan's studio or a client's kitchen table, not a boardroom.

**AI generation prompt:**
> Photorealistic close-up photograph. Several wood stain and finish sample boards fanned out on an unfinished oak table surface. Warm natural light from the left side. Samples show a range of wood tones from light ash and honey maple to rich walnut and ebony. A pencil and a small handwritten notepad visible at the edge of the frame. Shallow depth of field — sharpest in the center. Warm amber tones. No people, no faces. Feels like a working artisan consultation, not a retail display. Horizontal or slightly portrait crop.

**What to avoid:**
- Clinical white backgrounds or retail display lighting
- Too many samples making it look cluttered
- High-gloss commercial finish samples that look mass-produced

## CAMPAIGN 2: Cottage Furniture

**Campaign feeling:** Muskoka. Georgian Bay. Cottage country. Rustic, warm, built to last through humid summers and cold winters. The buyer owns or dreams of owning a cottage. They want furniture that feels like it belongs there — not a London showroom piece that got moved to the cottage. Heirloom quality, built for real life, built for generations.

**Color palette:** Honey and amber wood tones, deep forest greens, warm whites, natural linen, lake blues in the background (through windows or doors). No cool grays, no contemporary minimalism, no glossy finishes.

**The critical test for every image:** Would this make someone sitting in a Toronto condo think "I want that at my cottage"? If not, it's not right for this campaign.

---

### Image 1 — Hero Background (Dining Room Version)

**Placement:** Full-width hero section background (primary hero for the cottage furniture landing page)
**Dimensions:** 1920 x 1080px minimum, 16:9 horizontal

**What this image must do:**
This is the most important creative decision in the entire project. A visitor who searched "farmhouse dining table" or "muskoka furniture" lands here. In 2 seconds, this image either confirms "yes, this is exactly what I was looking for" or they bounce. The image is doing message match work before the headline is read. It must feel like authentic Ontario cottage country — not a Pinterest fantasy, not a showroom, not a city home.

**What to show:**
A large solid wood farmhouse dining table in a rustic Ontario cottage dining room. The table is the hero — long plank top with visible grain, seats 8–10, clearly handmade. The room around it says cottage: exposed wood ceiling beams, warm natural light through windows showing trees or water outside, simple linen curtains. Farmhouse-style chairs with natural wood frames. A simple centerpiece on the table — a bowl of fruit, a candle, nothing precious. Wide angle showing the full room. No people. Feels like Muskoka or Georgian Bay.

**AI generation prompt:**
> Photorealistic interior photography of a rustic Ontario cottage dining room. Large solid wood farmhouse dining table, wide plank top with visible natural grain, seats 8 to 10 people. Exposed wood ceiling beams. Warm afternoon light through wide windows showing trees or a lake in the background. Simple linen curtains. Farmhouse-style chairs with natural wood frames. A bowl of fruit or simple candle centerpiece on the table. No people. Warm amber and deep green tones. Wide angle showing the full room. 16:9 horizontal crop. Camera at seated eye level. Feels like a real Ontario cottage — Muskoka or Georgian Bay. Not staged, not a design magazine, not a showroom.

**What to avoid:**
- Any city or suburban interior feel
- Contemporary minimalist aesthetic
- White walls, no visible warmth
- Generic North American farmhouse that could be anywhere (needs to feel Ontario cottage specifically)
- AI artifacts on the table surface, beams, or window frames

### Image 4 — Farmhouse Dining Table Detail (Portfolio Anchor)

**Placement:** Portfolio section — "Our Dining Room Collection" hero image, first photo shown
**Dimensions:** 1200 x 900px

**What this image must do:**
This is the portfolio anchor for the highest-volume ad group in Campaign 2 (farmhouse dining table, 880–4,000 searches/month). Someone who searched "farmhouse dining table canada" or "rustic farmhouse dining table set" and scrolled to the portfolio needs to see their product immediately. This image does the final conversion work for the most valuable audience segment.

**Note:** Check the client drive first. If nothing suitable, request their most rustic or farmhouse-style dining table photo directly from Park Road. If the photo has the right piece but bad setting, lighting, or background, use AI to enhance it — place it into a cottage-feel environment, improve the lighting, or clean up the background. Only generate fully from scratch if the drive has nothing and the client cannot provide a photo. A real Park Road dining table in an AI-improved setting is the ideal outcome for this slot.

**What to show:**
A close to medium shot of a rustic solid wood farmhouse dining table — this time photographed in more detail than the hero. Long plank top, visible natural grain, farmhouse legs. Warm light that shows the wood texture beautifully. Simple rustic chairs. If possible, a slight cottage context in the background (wood wall, soft light). No people. This is the "product portfolio" version of Image 1 — more detail on the actual table.

**AI generation prompt:**
> Photorealistic photography of a solid wood rustic farmhouse dining table. Wide plank top with prominent natural grain — knots and character visible. Farmhouse-style legs, mortise and tenon construction visible at the corners. Warm directional side lighting that highlights the texture of the wood surface. Simple farmhouse chairs with natural wood frames. Background is soft and unfocused — a hint of wood paneling or a cottage wall. No people, no decorative objects on the table. Camera slightly above and to the side — showing both the top surface and the front legs. Warm honey and amber tones. Feels handcrafted and built to last.

**What to avoid:**
- Contemporary or modern dining table aesthetic
- High-gloss finish that reads as mass-produced
- Busy background that distracts from the table

### Image 5 — Craftsmanship Detail (Footer Accent)

**Placement:** Footer CTA section accent or mid-page visual break
**Dimensions:** 1200 x 600px minimum, wide landscape

**What this image must do:**
Same purpose as Campaign 1 Image 3 (proof of craft, quality signal) but with a more rustic character. The grain should be more prominent, the construction detail more visible. A visitor considering a $5,000+ purchase needs to see evidence of quality. This close-up is that evidence.

**What to show:**
Extreme close-up of a corner joint on a solid wood farmhouse dining table — mortise and tenon joinery visible. Deep walnut or oak grain. Warm side lighting. The joinery itself is the hero of the image — this is a detail that says "this was made by hand by someone who knows what they're doing." No people, no clutter.

**AI generation prompt:**
> Extreme close-up photorealistic photograph of the corner joint of a solid wood farmhouse dining table. Mortise and tenon joinery visible and prominent — the joinery is the focus of the image. Deep walnut or white oak grain. Warm amber directional side lighting from the left. No people, no clutter, no background distractions. Horizontal wide crop. Shallow depth of field with the sharpest point at the joint. Feels handcrafted, built to last, heirloom quality. Not factory-made. The wood grain should feel real — character, knots, variation.

**What to avoid:**
- Machine-cut or factory-finish appearance
- Cool or flat lighting that flattens the wood texture
- AI distortion of the wood grain or joinery geometry
