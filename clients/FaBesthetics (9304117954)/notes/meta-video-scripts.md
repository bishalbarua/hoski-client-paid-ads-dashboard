## CREATIVE TESTING PLAN

| Video | Angle | Funnel Stage | Tier | Launch Order | Primary Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Video 2 (fourth session UGC) | Results-in-progress, social proof | Cold | Tier 1 | Week 1 | Hook rate (3-sec view %), CTR, CPL |
| Video 5 (Jen testimonial) | Real client, trust | Cold | Tier 1 | Week 1 (concurrent) | Watch-through %, CTR, CPL |
| Video 1 (first session UGC) | Relatable journey start | Cold | Tier 1 | Week 1 (concurrent) | Hook rate, CPL |
| Video 4 (before/after energy) | Transformation | Cold | Tier 2 | Week 3–4 (after Tier 1 data) | CTR, CPL vs. Tier 1 |
| Video 3 (b-roll montage) | Aspirational / awareness | Cold — broader reach | Tier 2 | Week 3–4 | CPM, CTR (awareness signal) |

**Decision thresholds (at 1,000+ impressions per video):**
- Hook rate (3-sec view / impressions) below 15%: hook line needs a swap — the angle may still be right, just the opening isn't stopping the scroll
- CTR below 1%: weak creative-audience match — test a different angle
- CPL above $136 (2× Google blended CPA): pause and diagnose before cutting spend

**Winner call at Day 21:** Whichever video has the lowest CPL and highest hook rate gets 60% of impressions. The remaining budget splits across the next two performers. Add one new creative challenger in Week 5.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**For Videos 1 and 2 (AI UGC):**
- Use the same AI character generation prompt / tool for both so the face, skin tone, and style are consistent. Brief the designer: same person, two stages of her laser journey.
- Suggested AI tools: HeyGen, Creatify, Arcads, or similar for AI UGC character video
- If using real talent instead: a single day of filming covers both scripts back to back

**For Video 3 (b-roll montage):**
- Stock sources: Pexels, Storyblocks, Artgrid — search "laser hair removal," "smooth skin," "woman bathroom," "laser treatment gloves"
- Pink gloves in the laser shot are a brand-color win — source specifically or request from FaBesthetics if they have treatment footage

**For Video 4 (before/after energy split):**
- Same AI UGC character as Videos 1 and 2 works here — consistency builds familiarity across the campaign
- The energy shift (annoyed → relaxed) is the creative device. Direction note to editor: a quick cut or wipe with the title card is enough — no fancy transition needed

**For Video 5 (Jen testimonial):**
- Edit is critical. Jen's content is strong — the job is cleaning it up, not adding to it
- Do not add music under her voice — let her words land in silence
- Captions are mandatory: most Meta video is watched silent. Use auto-captions and clean them before export.
- Add the name bar ("Jen — FaBesthetics Client") within the first 5 seconds
