## Executive Summary

Norseman Construction is currently generating qualified leads at **$52 per lead on Google Ads** — a rate that is 65–87% below industry benchmarks for Toronto-market home renovation and custom home building. This is a strong, working system. The proposed $3,000/month combined budget for Google and Meta would require cutting spend from a channel that is already outperforming, while leaving Meta with an insufficient budget to generate any meaningful results.

**The short version:** $3,000/month is below the industry-recommended minimum for Google Ads alone in a competitive metro like Toronto — before Meta is even factored in. A well-funded campaign at the right budget level is capable of delivering an ROI that makes the investment look small against the project value of even a single closed deal.

- Option B: $1,500 Google + $1,500 Meta → Google lead volume drops from ~61/month to roughly 28/month, Meta may begin generating some signal but will still be constrained
- Either way: **fewer leads, less data, slower optimization, and more ground ceded to competitors**

## Section 3: What the Toronto Market Looks Like Right Now

### Competition Is Rising, Not Falling

- There are **1,499+ home builders and renovation contractors** listed on Houzz for the Toronto area alone
- Competitors' ad costs in construction rose **10.5% year-over-year** — roughly double the increase seen in other industries
- CPCs for high-intent renovation and custom home keywords in competitive metros now reach **$8–$18 per click for standard terms**, and **$100–$250 per click for high-value additions and whole-home queries**
- Norseman is currently operating at an efficiency that outpaces this benchmark — but only while the budget supports it

### Renovation Demand Is at a Sustained High

The GTA renovation market is in a strong cycle right now, driven by specific structural factors:

- **Housing affordability keeps people in place:** With Toronto home prices averaging $900,000–$2.5M for custom builds, many homeowners are choosing to renovate rather than move, driving sustained demand for contractors
- **Garden suites and laneway suites are surging:** Policy changes and housing affordability concerns made secondary suites one of the hottest construction categories in 2025 and continuing into 2026 — a category directly aligned with Norseman's services
- **Renovation spending is projected to keep growing** through the decade, per CMHC and industry analysts, as aging housing stock and lifestyle upgrades drive continued demand
- **Lower interest rates in 2026** are expected to unlock pent-up demand that was suppressed in 2023–2024

**This is precisely the market environment where a well-funded ad campaign captures outsized returns.** Competitors who maintain or increase budgets during a strong demand cycle lock in lead flow and customer acquisition while others pull back.

### What Competitors Are Spending

Leading Toronto-market custom home builders and renovation companies invest:

- **$3,000–$5,000/month on Google Ads alone** as a starting baseline (industry minimum recommendation for competitive markets)
- **$60–$100/day ($1,800–$3,000/month) on Meta** for awareness, retargeting, and lead generation
- **Combined: $5,000–$10,000+/month** is the norm for established players competing for the same high-value clients Norseman targets

A $3,000/month combined budget positions Norseman at the floor of what underfunded competitors spend on a single channel.

## Section 4: What the Campaigns Need to Perform

### Google Ads: The System Is Working — It Needs Scale, Not Cuts

The current Google campaigns are operating at a $52 CPL against an $88 target. This means there is room to:

1. **Increase the Custom Home Search campaign budget** from $50/day — impression share is likely being lost to budget, meaning Norseman is invisible for a portion of high-intent searches
2. **Scale the Home Renovations Search campaign** once it exits the learning phase (it is only a few weeks old)
3. **Investigate and clean up the PMax conversion overlap** to get accurate CPL data before scaling further

The right Google budget right now is **$3,000–$4,000/month** to capture the full opportunity the campaigns are already opening up.

### Meta Ads: The Budget Is Too Small to Work

Meta advertising for high-ticket home services requires a minimum spend for the algorithm to find the right audience and optimize delivery. The current $30/day is below this threshold — which explains the zero leads.

At **$60–$100/day ($1,800–$3,000/month)**, Meta can:
- Retarget website visitors who researched Norseman but did not submit a form
- Build lookalike audiences from existing clients and website visitors
- Run video creative and photo ads that build brand authority alongside the direct-response Google campaigns
- Generate leads at the industry CPL benchmark of $41–$45, potentially outperforming Google over time for awareness-driven renovation inquiries

Meta is a brand and trust channel as much as it is a lead channel for high-ticket services. Homeowners spending $200K–$2M on a build want to see the team, the work, and the story before they fill out a form. Meta is where that happens.

## Section 6: The Risk of Cutting Budget on a Winning Campaign

When a Google Ads campaign is performing at 41% below CPL target, the correct move is to scale — not cut. Budget reductions at this stage cause:

1. **Impression share loss:** Norseman becomes invisible for a portion of the highest-intent searches while competitors fill that gap
2. **Algorithm disruption:** Smart Bidding strategies (Target CPA, Maximize Conversions) depend on consistent spend to learn. Cuts reset the learning curve and inflate CPL temporarily even after budget is restored
3. **Competitor positioning:** Every dollar pulled back is market share handed to competing contractors. Once a competitor captures a client's attention, that client is difficult to recapture — especially for a high-consideration purchase like a custom home or full renovation
4. **Meta stays broken:** At $3,000 total, Meta cannot receive enough budget to generate any results, making it a pure cost with no return — which is the opposite of the problem

## Summary and Recommendation

Norseman is currently running a Google Ads campaign that is outperforming industry benchmarks by a wide margin. The Meta campaign has the infrastructure in place but is underfunded. Reducing the combined budget to $3,000/month would cut a working Google campaign and keep Meta in a non-functional state.

**The right question is not "how do we spend less?" but "how do we make the spend already generating qualified leads work harder?"**

Given that a single closed renovation client generates $75,000–$500,000+ in revenue, and the current CAC is approximately $520, there are very few investments in the business with a comparable return profile.

**Our recommendation: $5,000/month as the minimum, with a growth target of $6,000–$7,000/month.** This positions Norseman competitively in a high-demand Toronto market, captures the full Google opportunity already in motion, and gives Meta the budget it needs to become a genuine second lead channel.
