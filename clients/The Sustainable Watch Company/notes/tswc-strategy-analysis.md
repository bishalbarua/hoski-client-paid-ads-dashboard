## Landing Page Angle: "The Wood Is the Watch"

### The Problem

The current PDP is selling a wooden watch — the same thing every competitor does. Eco-friendly, sustainable, handmade: all floor-level claims that create zero differentiation and require the buyer to do the work of caring.

### The Angle

Lead with the wood species as the identity of the watch, not a feature of it. No competitor names their watches after wood species and then explains why the species matters. TSWC does this by naming convention already (Oak, Mahogany, Sandalwood, Banyan) — the PDP just needs to tell the story.

### What This Looks Like on the Page

```
H1: Mahogany. The wood that builds ships and boardrooms.
Subheadline: Now built into a watch that lasts as long.

[Watch photo]  [Star rating: 4.8 from 312 reviews]  [Add to cart — Free UK shipping]
```

Every species gets its own 1-line character statement:
- **Oak** — strength and endurance
- **Sandalwood** — calm and warmth
- **Teak** — durability and tradition
- **Banyan** — depth and longevity
- **Mahogany** — richness and gravitas

This is what people are actually buying: a piece of material that means something. No competitor is saying this.

### Why It Works for Both Channels

| Channel | Message Match |
|---|---|
| Google | Search query ("mahogany wood watch") → H1 ("Mahogany") = instant confirmation |
| Meta | Cold audience sees species personality and self-selects before reading description |

## Offer Angle: "Gift-Ready From the Moment It Arrives"

### The Problem with the Current Offer

15% off + quiz = two steps of friction before any value is delivered. It also trains the buyer to expect a discount, which undermines the premium positioning at £119-£299.

### The Angle

Replace the discount dependency with a tangible, verifiable value add: TSWC's own bamboo watch box (listed on site at £20, 5-star rated, 6 reviews) included free with every order. No code. No quiz. No friction.

The buyer can independently verify the value before they purchase — it's not "a gift box," it's a specific product with a price tag they can look up. That distinction is what makes this a conversion driver rather than a vague promise.

```
Every order includes:
✓ Bamboo watch box — worth £20, included free
✓ Species story card
✓ Free UK delivery
✓ Ships in 1-2 business days
```

### Why This Beats 15% Off

- 15% on a £149 watch = £22.35. Buyers at this price point are not price-sensitive to £22 — they're trust-sensitive and friction-sensitive.
- "Worth £20. Included free." is independently verifiable — the buyer can see the bamboo box listed on the site at £20 with 5-star reviews. Generic discount codes cannot be verified the same way.
- The bamboo box is on-brand (natural, eco-aligned) in a way a cardboard gift box is not. It reinforces the material story rather than undercutting it.
- Supports Father's Day: "Gift-wrapped in bamboo and ready to ship before Father's Day" is a complete, specific, premium offer — no imagination required.

### Test to Run Before Committing to Always-Free

| Version | Mechanic | Upside |
|---|---|---|
| Version A | Bamboo box auto-added free to every order | Higher conversion rate — zero friction |
| Version B | "Add the bamboo box for £10" at cart (50% off £20 listed price) | £10 additional revenue per order from buyers who want the box anyway |

Version A will likely convert higher. Version B recovers margin from buyers who were going to add it regardless. Run both for 2 weeks and let the data decide.

### Channel Application

| Channel | Hook | LP | Offer |
|---|---|---|---|
| Meta (cold) | "Each watch is named for the wood it's made from" | Species story H1 + character line | Free bamboo box (worth £20) + free UK shipping |
| Meta (warm) | "Still thinking about [species]?" | Same PDP | Urgency: ships in 1-2 days, free box included |
| Google Shopping | "[Species] Wood Watch Men — Handmade" | Species H1 — instant message match | Free bamboo box + free shipping visible above fold |
| Google Search | "wooden watch UK gift" | Father's Day angle LP | "Gift-wrapped in bamboo. Ships before 15 June." |

> **Creative asset needed:** Product photography of the watch sitting inside the open bamboo box. This is the Father's Day ad creative — no copy needed beyond the species name and the delivery deadline. Confirm with client whether this shot exists.

> **Client confirmation needed:** Actual return/warranty policy must be confirmed before putting a returns promise on the page.

## Gift Box: What Goes Inside

### Structure

The **bamboo watch box** (£20, sold on site, 5-star rated) is the container — it replaces the standard cardboard gift box every other brand ships in. Everything below goes inside it.

```
Bamboo Watch Box (the packaging)
│
├── The watch
├── Species story card        ← default, include from day one
├── Wood care card            ← default, include from day one
├── Microfiber care cloth     ← default, include from day one
└── Wood wax sachet           ← optional, add once supplier confirmed
```

The bamboo box is the headline of the offer because buyers can verify its £20 value on the site. The contents inside are what make the experience feel intentional rather than generic.

---

### Contents — Default (Include From Day One)

#### 1. Species Story Card (Highest Priority — Near-Zero Cost)

One printed card, species-specific. Different card for every wood type in the catalog.

- **Front:** Wood name in large type + simple illustration of the tree
- **Back:** 4-5 sentences about that specific wood

**Example (Mahogany):**
> A wood that built empires. Ships. Libraries.
> Mahogany has been prized for centuries for its density, its warmth, and the way it darkens beautifully with age — just like the things worth keeping.
>
> Your watch is made from sustainably sourced mahogany. It's meant to last.

**Why this matters:** This is the thing people photograph and share. It makes the unboxing feel intentional, not transactional. It costs pence to print and directly reinforces the core brand angle.

---

#### 2. Wood Care Card

Short, practical instructions: "How to keep your watch looking its best."

Positions the watch as an object worth maintaining, not a disposable purchase. Reinforces the "last watch you'll need to buy" anti-disposable angle.

---

#### 3. Microfiber Care Cloth

Standard at this price point. Branded with the TSWC mark if possible.

---

### Contents — Optional (Add Once Supplier Confirmed)

#### 4. Natural Wood Wax Sachet

A small beeswax or wood conditioning sachet for the strap.
- Tactile and on-brand
- Memorable and unexpected
- Signals craftsmanship and longevity
- Minimal cost in bulk

This does not need to be sourced before launch. The box ships complete without it and is upgraded once a supplier is locked in.

---

### What to Avoid

- Branded stickers or promotional inserts — kills the premium feel
- Plastic of any kind — obvious conflict with the brand
- A discount code for the next purchase inside the box — wrong message at this moment

## Evergreen Campaign Landing Page Strategy

### Why a Dedicated LP (Not the Collection Page)

The Shopify collection page is built for browsing — it has nav, footer, filters, and 32 products competing for attention. Cold Meta traffic arriving from the "named for its wood" brand concept ad needs to be sold on the idea before they browse. A dedicated LP controls the sequence: confirm the ad promise → introduce the offer → present the species choice → one CTA per watch to its PDP.

No nav. No footer links. No filter sidebar. One job: convert a cold visitor into a buyer.

---

### Page Type and URL

**Page type:** Brand + offer landing page (not a product page, not a collection page)
**Suggested URL slug:** `/collections/all-watches` (if overriding existing) or `/pages/find-your-wood`
**Platform note:** Build in Shopify using PageFly, Shogun, or GemPages — the standard Dawn theme collection page cannot be stripped of nav/footer without a custom section.

---

### Message Match Chain

The LP must confirm the promise made in the Meta ad within 3 seconds.

```
Meta ad hook → "Each watch is named for the wood it's made from"
     ↓
LP H1 → "Every watch is named for the wood it's made from."
     ↓
Subheadline → "Because the wood isn't just the material. It's the whole point."
     ↓
CTA → "Find your wood ↓"
```

If the ad and LP H1 don't mirror each other, the visitor's first instinct is "did I land in the right place?" and they leave. This chain must hold exactly.

---

### Above the Fold — Requirements

```
H1:         "Every watch is named for the wood it's made from."
Subhead:    "Because the wood isn't just the material. It's the whole point."
Visual:     Hero shot — bamboo box open, watch resting inside, species name visible
            (if this photo doesn't exist yet, this is the first asset to brief)
Offer bar:  "Free bamboo box (worth £20) + free UK delivery with every order"
Trust:      ★★★★★  [X] reviews  (specific number — not "hundreds of happy customers")
CTA:        "Find your wood ↓"  (scrolls to species section — soft CTA, not hard sell yet)
```

No navigation links. No header menu. TSWC logo only — links back to homepage for anyone who wants out, but doesn't advertise the exit.

---

### Page Sections (In Order)

#### Section 1: The Brand Promise (Above Fold)
*As above. Confirm the ad. Don't sell yet.*

---

#### Section 2: The Bamboo Box Offer
*Position this second — before the product grid. The visitor needs to know what they're getting before they choose.*

```
Headline:   "Every order arrives gift-ready."
Visual:     Flat lay — bamboo box open showing watch + story card + care cloth
Copy:       "Your watch ships in its own bamboo box (worth £20) with a species
             story card, care cloth, and wood care guide — included free.
             No code. No quiz. No extra steps."
Secondary:  "Ships in 1–2 business days."
```

This section answers the gift-buyer's primary objection before they've asked it: "Will this feel premium enough when they open it?"

---

#### Section 3: The Species Showcase
*The browsable section — but framed as a choice, not a grid.*

```
Headline:   "Choose your wood."
Subhead:    "Each species has its own character. Find the one that's yours."
```

Present 6–8 hero watches (best sellers from the men's and women's range). For each:
- Watch photo
- Species name in large type
- One-line character statement (e.g., "Oak — strength and endurance")
- Price
- CTA button: "Shop Oak →" (links to the Oak PDP)

Do not show all 32 products here. Show the 6–8 most likely to convert cold traffic, with a "See all [X] species →" link at the bottom for browsers who want more.

**Species selection for the showcase (confirm with client based on best sellers):**
Suggested: Oak, Mahogany, Sandalwood, Teak, Walnut, Pine (covers men's and women's price range)

---

#### Section 4: Social Proof
*Specific, not generic.*

```
Headline:   "What people say about their wood."
Format:     3–5 named reviews, each featuring the species the reviewer bought
            (e.g., "I got the Teak and three people asked me about it in the first week.")
Below:      Star rating aggregate with review count + link to full reviews
```

The reviews must name the species. "Great watch, very happy" does not convert. "Got the Mahogany for my husband and the story card made him actually emotional" converts.

---

#### Section 5: The Anti-Disposable Proof Point
*One short section. Handles the price objection before the visitor asks it.*

```
Headline:   "Built to outlast the habit of replacing watches."
Copy:       "The average person replaces their watch every 3 years.
             These are made to be the last one you'll need."
Visual:     Worn-in watch showing natural patina — not a pristine studio shot
```

---

#### Section 6: Final CTA Block
*Repeat the offer. One button.*

```
Headline:   "Find your wood."
Offer bar:  "Free bamboo box + free UK delivery. Ships in 1–2 days."
CTA:        "Shop all species →"  (links to collection page — full browse experience)
```

---

### Trust Signals Required

- Star rating with specific review count (not "hundreds" — the exact number)
- Named reviews that mention the species (request these specifically from existing customers)
- "Ships in 1–2 business days" — reduces anxiety for gift buyers
- Free bamboo box with verifiable £20 value (links to the bamboo box product page)
- TSWC logo only in header — no nav links that bleed traffic off the page

---

### Mobile Priority Notes

- H1 must be readable at 16px+ without pinching
- Bamboo box hero image must load under 3 seconds — compress before publishing
- Species CTA buttons must be thumb-friendly (minimum 44px height)
- Offer bar ("Free bamboo box + free UK delivery") must be visible without scrolling on a standard iPhone screen
- Species showcase: single column on mobile, two-column on tablet

---

### What to Avoid

- Navigation menu in the header — every nav link is a leak from a paid landing page
- Generic social proof ("customers love us") — kills the premium feel built by the species angle
- More than 8 species in the showcase section — choice paralysis at cold traffic stage
- Discount codes or % off anywhere on this page — it contradicts the premium bamboo box offer
- Studio-white product photography only — at least one lifestyle/worn shot per species in the showcase

### Angle Map

#### Saturated (100% floor — all competitors run this)

Every competitor in the wooden watch category leads with at least one of these. Running these angles alone is invisible.

- Eco-friendly / sustainable materials
- Natural wood / organic materials
- Handmade / artisan craftsmanship
- "Perfect gift for him" occasion

#### Common (50-80% — most competitors)

Table stakes for a competitive account, but not differentiating on their own.

- Minimalist / clean aesthetic
- Unique alternative to metal watches
- Generic durability / "built to last" claims
- Price-anchoring against luxury watch brands

#### Emerging (20-40% — some competitors beginning to test)

Worth testing — not yet saturated, but credibility requires the brand to be able to back it up.

- Anti-disposable / "buy once, buy right" positioning
- Founder or brand origin story
- Sustainability credentials with specificity (sourcing, certifications)

#### White Space (0-20% — nobody owns this in the category)

The two-part test for white space: (1) the client can credibly own it, and (2) buyers actually care about it. Both must be true.

| White Space Angle | Can TSWC Own It? | Do Buyers Care? | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Species identity — "named for the wood" | Yes. Their naming convention already does this. | Yes. People buying wooden goods care about the material, not just the category. | **Own it. Primary angle.** |
| Wood provenance — where each species grows and why it was chosen | Yes, if sourcing story exists. Confirm with client. | Yes. Conscious consumers want traceability. | **Own it, pending sourcing confirmation.** |
| Species personality matching — the wood tells you something about the person wearing it | Yes. Unique to TSWC's naming system. | Yes. Gift buyers especially want meaning, not just material. | **Own it. Primary gift angle.** |
| Species-specific long-tail SEO on Google | Yes. Zero competition currently. | Yes — search demand exists for specific wood types. | **Own it immediately via Shopping feed.** |

### Cross-Channel Synthesis

#### Google: Open Flanks

1. **Species-specific long-tail keywords** — "mahogany watch UK", "sandalwood wood watch", "teak watch men" — near-zero paid competition. TSWC can own the first page on these terms within weeks by rewriting Shopping feed titles to lead with the species name. Cost per click will be low; intent is high (the searcher knows what they want).

2. **Gifting occasion + wood** — "wooden watch Father's Day gift UK", "wood watch birthday gift" — competitors run generic gift messaging not tied to the material. TSWC's species gift angle ("Choose his tree") is the only occasion-specific, material-specific positioning in the category.

3. **"Sustainable watch UK"** — competitors landing on homepages or category pages with low Quality Scores. A species-specific landing page with clear message match would outperform on Ad Rank at lower CPC.

#### Meta: Open Flanks

1. **Species-personality creative** — no competitor runs a Meta ad that says "each watch is named for the wood it's made from." The hook is novel in the feed. It stops the scroll because it reframes what a watch is (not a time-keeping device, but a material choice).

2. **UGC unboxing with the species story card** — if the story card exists in the box, user-generated unboxing content becomes the brand's most powerful ad format. The card is the hero of the unboxing. No competitor has this asset.

3. **Anti-disposable angle** — "the last watch you'll need to buy" runs against the disposable fashion watch category (Casio, Fossil, Amazon basics). Competitors in the wooden category are not running this angle. It expands the competitive frame beyond wooden watches to all watches.

### Revised Ad Test Recommendations

> **Note:** All three tests revised from initial Cedar-only framing. TSWC has 32 SKUs across multiple wood species (men's £129-£299, women's £119-£149, straps £59, accessories). Ad tests must reflect the full catalog strategy, not a single SKU.

---

#### Ad Test 1: "Named for Its Wood" — Brand Concept (Multi-SKU)

**The angle:** The naming convention is the differentiator. Lead with the full catalog concept, not one product.

**Hook (video, first 3 seconds):**
> "Each of our watches is named for the wood it's made from."
> [Cut to: Oak. Mahogany. Sandalwood. Teak. Banyan.]
> "Because the wood matters."

**Hook (static):**
> "We don't name our watches after people. We name them after trees."

**Format:** Video preferred (species name reveal sequence). Carousel as alternative (one card per species, character line beneath each).

**Why TSWC can own this:** Their product naming already does it. The ad just makes it explicit. No competitor has 30+ SKUs named by species — this is not a claim any competitor can match without renaming their entire catalog.

**Google Shopping application:** Rewrite all 32 product titles to lead with species name.
Template: `[Species] Wood Watch [Men/Women] — Handmade Sustainable | TSWC`
Examples:
- "Mahogany Wood Watch Men — Handmade Sustainable | TSWC"
- "Sandalwood Watch Women — Eco Luxury | TSWC"
- "Teak Wood Watch Men — Handcrafted UK | TSWC"

---

#### Ad Test 2: "The Last Watch You'll Need to Buy" — Anti-Disposable Angle

**The angle:** Position against the disposable watch category, not just against other wooden watch brands. This expands the competitive frame and makes price objections irrelevant.

**Hook (video):**
> "The average person replaces their watch every 3 years."
> "This one is built to outlast that habit."

**Hook (static):**
> "Buy it once. Wear it for decades."

**Applies across full price range:** The anti-disposable argument works at £119 (Pine) and £299 (Banyan) equally. A higher price reinforces the "investment, not purchase" framing.

**Format:** Static image with bold copy overlay. Product photo should show a worn-in watch — patina, character — not a pristine studio shot.

---

#### Ad Test 3: "Which One Did You Get?" — Compliment Trigger (Social Proof)

**The angle:** The species name is a conversation starter. Tap into the social experience of wearing a watch that makes people ask questions.

**Hook (UGC-style video):**
> Person wearing a watch. Someone asks: "What kind of wood is that?"
> They say: "It's teak."
> "I didn't know you could make a watch out of teak."
> "Neither did I, until I found these guys."

**Why the species name is the punchline:** No competitor's customer can say "it's mahogany" or "it's sandalwood" in response to a question. TSWC's customers can. The name makes the conversation specific and memorable. Works for any SKU in the catalog — the species name changes, the social dynamic is the same.

**Format:** UGC video preferred. If no UGC exists yet, brief a creator to film this specific scenario.

---

#### Father's Day Angle (15 June 2026 — 10 Weeks Out)

**Campaign concept:** "Choose His Tree"

**The brief:** Father's Day is a gift-selection problem. The buyer doesn't know what to get — they want something meaningful, not generic. TSWC's species naming system turns the selection process into a meaningful act: "which wood represents him?"

**LP concept:** Species-based gift selector.
- "What kind of man is he?" → 3-4 personality questions → species recommendation
- This is the one context where a quiz ADDS conversion rather than removing it — because it's gift-oriented and helps an uncertain buyer commit
- End of quiz: recommended species + "Gift-ready: free box, ships by [date]"

**Ad hook options:**
- "Every dad is different. Every wood is too."
- "Choose his tree." (visual: lineup of species, each with character line)
- "The gift that tells him you thought about it."

**Urgency mechanism:** "Order by [date] for Father's Day delivery" — not a discount, a deadline. More powerful than % off for a considered gift purchase.
