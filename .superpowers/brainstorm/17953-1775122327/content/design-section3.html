Competitive Specialist
/competitive
REFACTOR

Purpose

Single source of competitive intelligence across both channels. Answers: what are competitors running, how are they positioned, where are the gaps you can exploit. Feeds both the Director and the Creative Strategist.

What changes from current

Currently two separate skills: /competitor-serp-scan (live SERP scraping) and /competitor-messaging-analysis (website positioning). After refactor: consolidated into one /competitive skill that does both in a single pass and delivers a unified report.

Inputs
  • Client URL + 2-3 competitor URLs
  • Target keywords / search terms
  • Client's current ad copy
  • Meta Ad Library (optional)
Outputs
  • Live SERP ad copy analysis
  • Competitor messaging audit
  • Positioning gap map
  • Angles you're missing
  • Saved to clients/[name]/analysis/
Wraps these existing agents
cross-competitive-intelligence meta-ad-library-intelligence

Absorbs + replaces: /competitor-serp-scan + /competitor-messaging-analysis

QA Manager
/qa
REFACTOR

Purpose

Adversarial review gate. Runs before any output reaches the client. Currently embedded inside the Marketing Director workflow — after refactor, also callable as a standalone skill so you can QA any output independently.

What changes from current

Currently only invoked through /marketing-director. After refactor: standalone /qa skill. You can point it at any document, analysis, or plan and get the 6-category review independently — without running the full Director workflow.

The 6 categories (unchanged)

1
Data Hallucinations — numbers cited not in the session data
2
Logical Gaps — recommendations that contradict their own stated thresholds
3
Cross-Specialist Contradictions — conflicts between two specialists' outputs
4
Strategy-Context Mismatch — correct advice that's wrong for this specific client
5
PPC Risk Flags — hidden efficiency cliffs, tracking gaps, data below thresholds
6
Completeness Failures — missing context, incomplete coverage, symptom vs. root cause

Output: PASS / CONDITIONAL PASS / FAIL with specific items to fix before delivery

Client Communication Manager
/client-comms
NEW

Purpose

Owns the full client-facing voice. Two jobs: (1) translate technical team output into polished client language, and (2) manage the ongoing relationship thread — drafting emails, status updates, responses to client questions, check-in notes.

Always references

  • client-info.md — for tone, goals, relationship history, what this client cares about
  • Most recent report or analysis — for facts and numbers
  • PPC rule: zero jargon in client-facing output (no QS, IS, eCPC unless client is technical)

Relationship modes

  • Proactive: Monthly narrative, weekly status note, campaign launch announcement
  • Reactive: Response to client question, concern, or escalation
  • Milestone: New campaign live, budget change, strategic pivot explanation

Translation examples

Internal: "IS lost to rank dropped from 28% to 41% — bid floor likely below competitive threshold"
→
Client: "Your ads are showing up less often in top positions. We're adjusting bids to get you back in front of more high-intent searches."
Internal: "tROAS bid strategy in learning period after +15% target adjustment — expect 7-10 day performance variance"
→
Client: "We made a strategic adjustment to your bidding last week. Performance may look slightly different for a few days while the system recalibrates — this is normal and expected."
Internal: "/weekly-check output — P1: conversion tag firing 0 events on thank-you page since 3/28"
→
Client: "We caught a tracking issue that we're fixing today. No leads were missed — the form still worked — but our reporting wasn't recording them correctly."
