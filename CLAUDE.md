# Höski Marketing Manager — Operating Doctrine

Every agent, skill, and analysis in this system operates under the following defaults. These are not guidelines to reference — they are the default way we think and work.

---

## The Primary Variable Rule

**Meta Ads:** Creative is the primary variable. Targeting is commoditized — broad targeting works, Advantage+ works, the algorithm finds the audience. All optimization effort goes to angles, hooks, formats, and creative volume. When Meta underperforms, look at creative first.

**Google Ads:** Landing page is the primary variable. Keywords constrain intent, ad copy is bounded by character limits — but what happens after the click determines whether the business makes money. When Google underperforms and tracking is confirmed healthy, the landing page is the first place to look.

---

## Unit Economics First

Before recommending any spend, scaling any campaign, or setting any target, the following must be known:

- **AOV** — average order value (or average transaction value for lead gen)
- **COGS** — cost of goods sold including shipping and fulfillment
- **Contribution Margin** — what's left after COGS
- **Breakeven CPA** — (AOV × margin%) = the maximum you can pay and not lose money
- **Target CPA** — 70-80% of breakeven (never at breakeven — the buffer is the profit)
- **LTV:CAC Ratio** — below 2:1 is a warning, below 1:1 is a fire

If these numbers are not in `client-info.md`, surface that gap immediately. Never set a CPA target without running the math.

---

## Metrics Hierarchy

**MER (Media Efficiency Ratio) = Total Revenue / Total Ad Spend**

MER is the business truth for DTC/e-commerce clients. It is the lead metric for any business-level decision about whether marketing is working.

Platform ROAS is for intra-platform optimization only: which campaign to scale, which to kill, whether a bid strategy is working. Never use platform ROAS to make a business-level spend decision.

If MER is healthy and platform ROAS looks weak: attribution is noisy, the business is fine.
If MER is declining and platform ROAS looks strong: there is a real problem — likely over-retargeting and under-prospecting.

---

## Funnel Architecture Defaults (DTC)

Budget splits for DTC/e-commerce clients unless data says otherwise:

- **60-70% Prospecting** — awareness and new customer acquisition
- **20-25% Retargeting** — convert warm traffic
- **10-15% Retention** — email, SMS, loyalty (where margin compounds)

First-order profitability is the North Star. The goal from ads is to acquire customers who are profitable on the first order. Retention channels (email, SMS) are where repeat purchase margin compounds.

---

## Calendar Defaults

**Tier 1 Events for DTC** (BFCM, Q5, Valentine's Day, Mother's Day): require a 4-week runway.
- Week -4: Creative production begins, briefs to creators
- Week -3: Email sequences drafted, landing pages built
- Week -2: Creatives in review, campaigns structured, audiences built
- Week -1: Everything scheduled, test runs live, final QA
- Week 0: Launch, monitor hourly for first 48 hours

If a Tier 1 event is within 4 weeks and prep has not started: flag it immediately. Do not wait to be asked.

**Q5 (Dec 26 - Jan 7):** Never a pause period. CPMs crater while consumers are home with gift cards. Scale into it.

Reactive marketing is not acceptable. If work is happening the week before an event, something went wrong.

---

## Weekly Operating Rhythm

- **Monday:** Review weekend performance. Kill underperformers. Scale winners. Plan creative needs for the week.
- **Tuesday-Wednesday:** Creative production. Brief creators. New assets in pipeline.
- **Thursday:** Launch new test batch (minimum 4-5 new creatives for DTC — adjusted by vertical, see service business track below).
- **Friday:** Performance review. Update hypothesis tracker. Update marketing calendar. Flag red flags.

---

## Creative Testing Defaults (DTC)

- Each creative gets **$100-150 budget**, minimum **72-hour run** before any kill decision
- Measure: outbound CTR, hook rate (3-second video view %), cost per result
- Kill anything below threshold. Move winners to Evergreen.
- Budget increase on winners: **20-30% every 3-5 days**, never overnight

---

## Hypothesis Tracker Default

Every test must have these five fields documented:

| Week | Hypothesis | Test | Result | Learning | Next Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

No test without a hypothesis. No result without a learning. No learning without a next action.

---

## Scaling Defaults

- Budget increases: 20-30% every 3-5 days on winning campaigns. Never overnight.
- CPA spike threshold: if CPA is more than 30% above target for 3+ consecutive days during scaling, pull back and diagnose before continuing.
- **Cost cap strategy for stable scaling:** Set CPA or ROAS target, give the campaign 3-5x the normal daily budget. It only spends when it can hit the target. This prevents runaway spend during scaling.

---

## 5 Stages of Awareness

Every piece of creative targets a specific awareness stage. Default map:

| Stage | What They Know | What the Creative Does |
|---|---|---|
| Unaware | Nothing about the problem | Educate. Surface the problem. |
| Problem Aware | They have a problem | Agitate the problem. Introduce that a solution exists. |
| Solution Aware | Solutions exist | Position the product as the best solution. |
| Product Aware | The product exists | Differentiate. Social proof. Offer. |
| Most Aware | Ready to buy | Close. Urgency. Best deal. |

Cold prospecting creative targets stages 1-3.
Retargeting creative targets stages 4-5.

Default diagnosis when prospecting underperforms: check whether creative is stuck at stages 4-5 and skipping the awareness-building stages.

---

## Red Flags Requiring Immediate Action

| Red Flag | What It Means | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| CPA rising 3 weeks in a row | Creative fatigue or market shift | New creative batch. Test new angles. Check competitors. |
| MER declining while platform ROAS is stable | Over-retargeting. Not acquiring new customers. | Check new customer %. Shift budget to prospecting. |
| AOV dropping | Customers buying cheaper items or less per order | Review product mix. Test bundles. Check offer structure. |
| Email open rates below 20% | List health issues or subject line problems | Clean list. Segment. A/B test subject lines. |
| Client asking "what are you doing?" | Communication gap | Send a proactive update immediately. Do not wait for the next scheduled report. |

---

## Decision Mindset

- **Data over feelings.** If you cannot point to the specific data that supports a decision, it is a guess, not a decision.
- **Test, do not debate.** Opinions are free. Data costs $150 and 72 hours.
- **Think like an owner.** Every dollar spent is a dollar the client earned. Treat it that way.
- **Speed wins.** The team that tests 20 creatives a month beats the team that tests 5. Do not be reckless, but do not be slow.

## Service Business Track

75% of Höski clients are service businesses: dental, medical, chiropractic, legal, med spa, construction, high-ticket retail (GDM, Park Road, EJPR), and B2B. The Five Laws hold for all of them. The execution layer beneath each law is different.

**Before applying any default, determine which track the client is on:**

- **DTC/E-commerce track:** Shopify or equivalent. Online purchase is the conversion. MER applies. Use the defaults above.
- **Service business track:** Conversion is a call, form submission, appointment booking, or consultation request. MER does NOT apply. GHL/CRM pipeline is the bottom of the funnel. Use the service business defaults below.
- **High-Ticket Consultation-First Retail (Track C):** Physical products with consultation-gated closing (GDM, Park Road, EJPR). DPA and Google Shopping apply, but retargeting windows are 60-90 days and the primary conversion event is a consultation or showroom visit, not an online purchase.

---

### Law 1 (Service Business): Know the Math Before You Spend

The DTC formula (AOV × margin = max CPA) does not work for a dental practice, law firm, or med spa. Use this model instead:

```
Max CPL = Avg Revenue per New Client × Lead-to-Close Rate × Show Rate × Gross Margin %
Target CPL = Max CPL × 0.75
```

**The four questions to ask every service client in Week 1:**
1. What is your average revenue per new client or patient?
2. What percentage of people who book a consultation actually show up (show rate)?
3. Of the people who show up, what percentage become paying clients (lead-to-close rate)?
4. What is your approximate gross margin on that service?

**High-ticket context:** For clients with average case values above $5,000 (Voit Dental at $29,900, Park Road at $5k-$15k+, GDM custom rings), the CPL ceiling is naturally higher. A $200 CPL that closes into a $15,000 case is a 75:1 return. Do not apply DTC-calibrated CPL anxiety to these accounts.

If the client does not know these numbers: that is the first problem to solve. Do not run ads until a defensible CPL target exists.

### Law 2 (Service Business): Creative Volume by Vertical

The 4-5 creatives per 72-hour cycle is only valid for DTC. Service businesses have lower conversion volumes and longer consideration cycles. Adjust:

| Client Type | Volume Per Cycle | Test Duration | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| DTC e-commerce | 4-5 | 72 hours | High purchase volume, fast data |
| Med spa / aesthetics | 2-3 | 5-7 days | Longer consideration, lower volume |
| Dental (general) | 2-3 | 5-7 days | Appointment-based, lower daily conversions |
| Dental (high-ticket implants) | 1-2 | 10-14 days | $29,900 decision, very low daily volume |
| Chiropractic / functional medicine | 2-3 | 5-7 days | Condition-specific, slower data |
| Legal | 1-2 | 10-14 days | Case intake is low-volume |
| Construction / home renovation | 2-3 | 7-10 days | Seasonal, long project cycle |
| High-ticket furniture / jewelry | 1-2 | 10-14 days | Long consideration, low conversion volume |

**Service business creative angles (in addition to the DTC angles):**

| Angle | When to Use |
|---|---|
| Provider credentials + trust | Medical, legal, dental — doctor background, years in practice |
| Before / after outcome | Dental, aesthetics, chiropractic — compliantly framed patient results |
| "You're not alone" empathy | Chronic pain, mental health — normalize the problem before the solution |
| Social proof / volume | Any service — "500+ 5-star reviews", "8,000 consultations completed" |
| Cost comparison | High-ticket — "Save $20,000+ vs. ClearChoice" |
| Process demystification | Consultation-gated service — "Here's exactly what happens at your first appointment" |
| Financing availability | High-ticket dental, furniture — "As low as $X/month" |
| Founder / practitioner on camera | Med spa, boutique dental — the provider IS the brand |

### Law 3 (Service Business): Cost Per Qualified Outcome Replaces MER

MER requires a unified revenue feed (Shopify, etc.). Most service clients cannot produce this — dental practices have insurance billing cycles, law firms have case settlements months after the click.

**The four numbers that replace MER for service businesses:**

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| Cost Per Lead (CPL) | Platform efficiency — the input |
| Show Rate | Lead quality. Below 60% means something is wrong upstream. |
| Lead-to-Close Rate | Sales and offer effectiveness. If low, the problem is not the ads. |
| Cost Per Acquired Client | The real number. CPL / (show rate × close rate). This is what to optimize toward. |

**Service business dashboard (replaces DTC MER dashboard):**

| Metric | Source | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Target CPL | Unit economics calculation | The ceiling |
| Actual CPL | Platform + CallRail | Are we hitting target? |
| Show Rate | GHL pipeline | Are leads showing up? |
| Lead-to-Close Rate | GHL pipeline | Is the funnel working post-lead? |
| Cost Per Acquired Client | Combined platforms / GHL | Is the business growing from ads? |
| Call Duration Distribution | CallRail | Are calls the right length to be qualified? |
| Channel Split | Google / Meta / LSA | Which platform drives qualified outcomes? |

If platform CPL looks healthy but show rate is below 50%: lead quality problem. If show rate is healthy but close rate is low: sales or offer problem, not an ads problem.

### Law 4 (Service Business): Tier 1 Events by Vertical

The 4-week prep rule applies to all of these. The dates change entirely.

**Dental:** January (new insurance year — highest intent period), April/May (pre-summer cosmetic peak), September (back-to-school / end-of-year insurance utilization). BFCM only if running a specific offer on elective cosmetic services.

**Medical / Chiropractic / Functional Medicine:** January (new insurance year + health resolutions), September (back-to-school return to routine), November (pre-deductible-reset push — patients want to use remaining benefits before year-end).

**Med Spa / Aesthetics:** April/May (pre-summer body treatment season), October/November (pre-holiday facial and skin peak), Valentine's Day (Tier 1), Mother's Day (Tier 1).

**Legal:** No universal Tier 1. Map to case types. Personal injury peaks in winter (accidents) and summer (travel). Family law peaks in January (post-holiday separation) and September (back-to-school transitions).

**Construction / Home Services:** March-April (spring season start — highest intent), August-September (fall renovation window), January (planning season — budget reset).

**High-Ticket Retail (jewelry, furniture):** Valentine's Day (Tier 1 for jewelry), Mother's Day (Tier 1 for jewelry and home decor), pre-holiday November (gift and home refresh season).

### Law 5 (Service Business): The GHL Pipeline Is Part of the Funnel

For service businesses, what happens after the form submission or phone call is as important as what happens before it. The GHL pipeline is not a separate system — it is the bottom of the funnel.

**Service Business Funnel Architecture:**

```
TOP OF FUNNEL (50-60% of total spend)
Goal: Get qualified prospects to raise their hand
Campaigns: Google Search (high-intent terms) + Meta awareness
Budget: 50-60% of total spend

MIDDLE (25-30% of total spend)
Goal: Convert expressed interest into a booked appointment
Channels: Retargeting ads + GHL automated sequences (SMS/email)

BOTTOM (10-15% — GHL operational, not ad spend)
Goal: Ensure booked leads show up and convert
Channels: GHL reminders, re-engagement sequences, review requests
```

**Retargeting windows for service businesses** (longer than DTC's 7-14 days):

| Vertical | Retargeting Window |
|---|---|
| General dental / medical / aesthetics | 30 days |
| High-ticket dental (implants) | 60-90 days |
| Legal | 60 days |
| Construction / home renovation | 90 days |
| High-ticket furniture / jewelry | 60-90 days |

**Budget split defaults by vertical** (replace the 60-70% Meta default for service clients):

| Client Type | Google | Meta | LSA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dental (general) | 40% | 30% | 30% |
| Dental (high-ticket implants) | 50% | 40% | 10% |
| Medical / chiropractic | 50% | 40% | 10% |
| Med spa / aesthetics | 30% | 60% | 10% |
| Legal | 70% | 20% | 10% |
| Construction / home renovation | 60% | 30% | 10% |
| High-ticket retail | 40% | 50% | 0% |
| B2B (Hoski.ca) | 40% | 30% | 0% (LinkedIn 30% if budget allows) |

**Landing page requirements for service businesses** (additions to the standard 5 non-negotiables):
- Provider credentials and trust signals within the first scroll: years of experience, license/certification, specific training, number of patients/cases handled. Generic "experienced" claims do not count.
- Objection handling: address cost, process, and "is this right for me?" before the prospect has to ask. For high-ticket services the objection is almost always one of: cost, process fear, or trust in the provider.
- For medical / legal: compliance-reviewed copy before launch.

### Law 6: Lead Quality Is the Metric (Service Businesses)

A lead is not a conversion. A cheap lead that never shows is worse than an expensive lead that converts. The job is not to generate leads — it is to generate qualified pipeline.

**Define "qualified" before launch.** Before the first campaign goes live, answer with the client: what makes a lead worth our time? Document it in client-info.md.

**Speed-to-lead is a campaign health metric.** The probability of qualifying a lead drops by 80% if response time exceeds 5 minutes. When show rate is low, check follow-up speed in GHL before adjusting the campaign.

**Recommended follow-up protocol for all service business clients:**
- Immediate automated SMS + email from GHL within 60 seconds of form submission
- Human follow-up attempt within 5 minutes during business hours
- 3-attempt sequence over 48 hours if no response
- If no contact after 48 hours: move to long-term nurture sequence, not dead lead

**Offline conversion import (for accounts with sufficient volume):** Shift campaign optimization from "form submitted" to a downstream event (appointment attended, consultation completed) exported from GHL and imported back into Google Ads and Meta. Requires approximately 30+ conversion events per month per platform to be statistically useful. This trains the algorithm to find people who show up, not just people who fill out forms.

### Compliance Defaults (Healthcare and Legal)

**For all healthcare clients (dental, medical, chiro, med spa, plastic surgery):**
- No guaranteed outcomes in copy: "cure," "eliminate," "fix permanently" are all high-risk. "Many of our patients report significant relief" is compliant.
- No diagnosis implication in Meta copy: "Do you have peripheral neuropathy?" is non-compliant. "Are you experiencing chronic foot or leg pain?" is safer.
- Meta targeting: broad targeting or location-based only. No condition-based interest targeting (cannot target "back pain" or "diabetes" as interests). No lookalikes built from health-condition-specific engagement data.
- Before/after imagery must accurately represent typical patient outcomes, not idealized results.
- When in doubt: run the ad copy past the client's office manager before publishing.

**For all legal clients (Anand Desai Law):**
- State bar rules apply to legal advertising. Most bars prohibit direct claims of being "the best" without substantiation.
- Most bars require: "Attorney Advertisement" disclaimer.
- All ad copy for legal clients must be reviewed by the client before it goes live. Get email confirmation. This protects the agency.
